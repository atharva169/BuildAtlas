# BuildAtlas GenAI — Technical Architecture (For Non-Technical Audiences)

---

BuildAtlas uses a **hybrid approach** that combines traditional calculation engines with Google's Gemini AI — and the split is intentional. Think of it like a hospital: when you go for a blood test, you want the lab machine to count your blood cells precisely — you don't want a doctor guessing. But when you sit down to discuss the results, you want a doctor who can explain what the numbers mean, suggest treatment options, and answer your questions in plain language. BuildAtlas works the same way. The **calculation engines** (cost estimation, scheduling, what-if simulation) are the lab machines — they use verified CPWD government rates, Indian Standard code requirements, and city-specific construction data to produce precise numbers. The **AI layer** (Gemini) is the doctor — it reads the numbers, writes risk narratives like "TMT steel is your primary cost risk due to recent global price volatility," recommends materials with IS code citations, and answers natural-language questions through the copilot.

This hybrid design solves a critical problem with using AI alone for construction planning: **you can't afford hallucinated numbers**. If a pure AI system says "your house will cost ₹1.5 Crore" when the real answer is ₹2.1 Crore, someone makes a financial decision based on wrong data. That's unacceptable in construction. By keeping all calculations rule-based and deterministic, we guarantee that the numbers are as accurate as the underlying data (CPWD rates, city multipliers, quality grades). The AI is used only where creativity and explanation add value — not where precision is required. The result is a system that's both **trustworthy** (every number is traceable to a formula and a data source) and **intelligent** (the explanations, narratives, and recommendations feel like talking to an experienced construction advisor). The entire system runs on a React frontend that loads instantly with pre-computed data, a Python FastAPI backend that handles AI requests, and an offline-first architecture that ensures the demo works perfectly even without an internet connection.
