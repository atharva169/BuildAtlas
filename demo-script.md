## ACT 3: THE COST ENGINE (1:30 – 2:15)

**[1:30] Click "Estimator" in sidebar**

> "Now — cost estimation. And this is where BuildAtlas diverges from every existing tool."

**[1:35] Point to the three P10/P50/P90 cards**

> "We don't give you one number. We give you three. Best case — 1.55 Crore, where everything goes perfectly, you get bulk discounts, and the monsoon is mild. Most likely — 1.84 Crore. And worst case — 2.31 Crore, accounting for steel price spikes, delays, and re-work. No construction tool in India gives you confidence bands."

> "Below that, a 12-line Bill of Quantities. Excavation, RCC structure, TMT steel, brickwork, plastering, flooring, electrical, plumbing, painting, fixtures — every trade. With exact amounts. The system flags TMT steel as the primary variance driver because it's 14.8% of total cost and historically the most volatile material."

**[2:05] Point to city rate and multipliers**

> "The cost engine uses Bengaluru-specific CPWD rates, quality grade multipliers, and floor-count structural scaling. It's not a generic calculator — it's city-aware, code-aware, and market-aware."

## ACT 4: THE WOW MOMENT — WHAT-IF SANDBOX (2:15 – 3:00)

**[2:15] Click "What-If" in sidebar**

> "Here's my favourite feature. The What-If Sandbox."

**[2:20] Slowly drag the Steel slider to +15%**

> *(Pause and let the numbers update in real time)*
>
> "Steel prices went up 15 percent last monsoon season. Watch what happens. *(drag slider)* The cost jumps from 1.84 Crore to 1.93 Crore. That's 4 lakh rupees — from steel alone. And look — the risk score goes up. Schedule extends by a week. All of this in 200 milliseconds."

**[2:40] Drag Labour slider to +10%**

> "Now labour rates increase too. We're now at 1.98 Crore. The system shows you a breakdown — steel contributed 4 lakhs, labour contributed 2 lakhs, and the timeline shift added another lakh in overhead costs."

**[2:55] Click "Reset All"**

> "A quantity surveyor would need a day to run these scenarios. BuildAtlas does them live, as many times as you want."

## ACT 5: SCHEDULE + MONSOON (3:00 – 3:30)

**[3:00] Click "Schedule" in sidebar**

> "The schedule engine is monsoon-aware. Bengaluru gets its South-West monsoon from June through September. The system automatically adds buffer weeks to outdoor phases — excavation, superstructure — and keeps indoor work like MEP and finishes unaffected."

**[3:15] Point to the monsoon badge (8WK MONSOON) and the Gantt chart**

> "See the monsoon badge — 8 weeks of weather buffer are already baked in. This is critical. In India, monsoon is the number one cause of construction delays. We handle it by default."

**[3:25] Point to Delay Cascade section**

> "And if a phase still gets delayed? The cascade simulator shows you exactly which downstream phases get pushed, by how many days, and the cost impact. Foundation delayed by 3 weeks? Superstructure shifts, MEP shifts — total 14 extra days, 3.2 lakhs in extra overhead."

**[3:30] Click "Reverse Plan" in sidebar**

> "Now here's where we flip the workflow. Reverse Planning. Most tools go from design to cost. We go from budget to design."

> "Tell the system — 'I have 50 lakhs and 14 months.' It generates three feasible configurations. Economy — basic finishes, reduced carpet area, but structurally compliant. Standard — balanced approach. Premium — full specs but tight timeline. Each option shows exactly what's included and what's cut."

**[3:50] Open AI Copilot panel (click the panel or FAB)**

> "The AI Copilot is project-aware. It's not ChatGPT. It knows your city, your area, your budget, your start date."

**[3:55] Type: "Can I use AAC blocks instead of clay bricks?"**

> "Watch — it responds with Bengaluru-specific data. AAC blocks are lighter, better thermal insulation, and save 2.1 lakhs. It cites IS 2185 Part 1, gives you the cost comparison, and mentions 85% availability in Bengaluru from Ultratech and Magicblocks."

## HANDLING QUESTIONS

| If they ask... | Do this... |
|---|---|
| A technical question | Answer directly with specifics (IS codes, tech stack) |
| "Is this real data?" | "The cost rates are from CPWD 2024 and city-specific market surveys" |
| "Can I try it?" | Hand them the laptop, let them adjust sliders |
| Something you don't know | "That's a great question — in our Phase 2 roadmap we're exploring..." |
| An adversarial question | Stay calm, acknowledge the limitation, pivot to what works |

> **Golden Rule**: Never get defensive. If a feature is limited, say "Currently it handles X, and extending to Y is planned." Never oversell.
